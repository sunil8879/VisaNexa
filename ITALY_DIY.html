Italy PR: DIY vs AGENCY TRUTH

🚨 THE "CLICK DAY" EXPLOITATION

Italy's work visa system is based on an annual quota called the Decreto Flussi. Because slots are limited and issued on a "first-come, first-served" basis (Click Day), agencies in India charge ₹5L to ₹15L claiming they have "reserved slots." In reality, no one can reserve a slot. You are paying lakhs for a 1-second lottery that is legally free.

1. End-to-End Immigration Lifecycle
Technical Glossary:

• Decreto Flussi: The annual decree that sets the number of foreign workers allowed into Italy.
• Nulla Osta: The official "No Impediment" (Authorization) document issued by the Italian Prefecture.
• Sportello Unico per l'Immigrazione (SUI): The unified immigration office where employers apply.
• Permesso di Soggiorno: The mandatory Residence Permit card applied for after landing in Italy.

3. Timeline & Critical Risks

TIMELINE

• Click Day Wait: Varies (Decree usually issued late in year)

• Nulla Osta Process: 2-5 months (Official queue)

• Visa Stamping: 15-30 days

Note: Italy has a massive backlog. If an agent promises you a visa in "30 days," they are lying or providing a fake document.

THE "FLUSSI" RISKS

• Fake Nulla Osta: Agents providing photoshopped authorization letters.

• Seasonal Trap: Being sent on a 6-month seasonal visa but promised a permanent job.

• Sponsor Blacklist: If the Italian employer hasn't paid taxes, your Nulla Osta is revoked.

4. What Actually Decides Success in Italy?
