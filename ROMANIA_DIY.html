Romania Work Visa: DIY vs AGENCY TRUTH

🚨 THE ROMANIAN WORK PERMIT REALITY

In Romania, a Work Permit (Aviz de angajare) costs the employer only €100. Agencies in India often charge ₹4 Lakhs to ₹10 Lakhs for "Romania Job Packages." Most of these are for manual labor, construction, or factory roles where the "Job Spot" is being sold illegally. If you pay an agent, you are often paying for a "fake" sponsorship that leads to visa cancellation at the border.

1. End-to-End Immigration Lifecycle
Technical Glossary:

• IGI: General Inspectorate for Immigration (The body that approves the work permit).
• Aviz de angajare: The official Work Permit document issued to the employer.
• Type D/AM Visa: The long-stay visa required to enter Romania for employment.
• Permis de ședere: The mandatory Residence Permit card applied for after arrival.

🧾 DIY Workflow

3. Timeline & Critical Risks

TIMELINE

• Job Hunting: 2-5 months (Direct apps)

• IGI Work Permit: 3 months (Standard)

• Visa Stamping: 15 working days

Note: Romania has joined Schengen for air/sea. This means security checks are now 10x stricter. Any agent promising a "100% guarantee" is ignoring the new EU-wide security protocols.

CRITICAL RISKS

• The "Schengen Lie": Agents saying you can work in Germany on a Romanian visa. This is 100% ILLEGAL.

• Labour Scams: Paying ₹5 Lakhs for a job that pays only ₹40,000/month in Romania.

• Container Housing: Being forced to live in poor, crowded accommodation provided by the "Visa Supplier."

4. What Actually Decides Success?
1. Technical VocationAutomotive mechanics, welders, and skilled cooks are the most sponsored roles in Romania.
2. English ProficiencyWhile not legally required like Australia, basic English is needed to clear technical interviews with HR.
3. Clean Legal RecordA clean PCC (Police Clearance) is non-negotiable for IGI approval.
4. Direct RecruitmentReal Romanian firms in Timișoara or Cluj prefer direct hiring to avoid the legal liability of third-party agents.
