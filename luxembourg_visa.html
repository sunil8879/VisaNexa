🇱🇺 LUXEMBOURG IMMIGRATION – GENERAL PROCESS (2026)

🧭 1. CHOOSE YOUR VISA TYPE

First, decide the correct category:

  • Work Visa (Skilled Worker / EU Blue Card)
  • Student Visa
  • Job Seeker Visa
  • Family Reunification Visa
  • Self-Employment / Startup Visa
  • Investor Visa
  • Internship / Trainee Visa
  • Long-Stay Visa (Type D – National Visa)

👉 Luxembourg is strict: your visa type must match your purpose exactly.

⚠️ 2. PRE-APPROVAL (MANDATORY STAGE)

For most long-stay visas (especially work), you must apply for authorisation to stay BEFORE entering Luxembourg. This is handled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Immigration Directorate).

📄 3. APPLY FOR AUTHORISATION TO STAY

Submit documents to Luxembourg immigration. Requirements: Passport, CV, job contract or admission letter, educational certificates, proof of funds, criminal record certificate, and health insurance. Processing takes 2–3 months typically.

✅ 4. RECEIVE “AUTHORISATION TO STAY”

If approved, you receive the “Autorisation de séjour”. This is your pre-approval allowing you to enter the country.

🏛️ 5. APPLY FOR TYPE D VISA (ENTRY VISA)

Apply at the Embassy of Luxembourg or the Belgian Embassy (which represents Luxembourg in countries like India). Submit your authorisation letter, passport, visa form, and travel proof.

✈️ 6 & 7. TRAVEL & DECLARATION OF ARRIVAL

After visa approval, enter Luxembourg. Within 3 days of arrival, you must register at the local commune (city hall).

🧪 8. MEDICAL CHECK + BIOMETRICS

You must complete a medical examination by an approved doctor and submit biometrics (photo + fingerprints) for your identity registration.

🪪 9. APPLY FOR RESIDENCE PERMIT

At the Immigration Directorate, convert your entry visa into a Residence Permit Card. This card is usually valid for 1–3 years and is renewable.

🧠 SUMMARY FLOW: Job/Admission → Authorisation to Stay → Type D Visa → Entry → Registration → Medical + Biometrics → Residence Permit
