🇫🇷 GENERAL PROCESS FOR FRANCE IMMIGRATION (2026)

🧭 1. IDENTIFY YOUR PURPOSE

First decide the correct category based on your needs:

  • Study: For University degrees or exchange programs.
  • Work: For salaried employees or "Talent Passports".
  • Business: For Entrepreneurs or Investors.
  • Family: For joining a spouse or dependent in France.
  • Temporary Stay: For Visitors, Training, or Internships.

📩 2. GET ADMISSION / JOB / SPONSORSHIP

Depending on the visa, you must secure the core requirement: University admission (+ Campus France), a valid job offer/contract, or a detailed business plan.

🔍 3. CHECK VISA REQUIREMENTS

Prepare your eligibility: Passport (6+ months), financial proof (bank statements), accommodation proof in France, health insurance, and academic documents.

🌐 4 & 5. OFFICIAL REGISTRATION & APPLICATION

Register on Campus France (for students) and the France-Visas portal. Complete the online form, select short or long-stay, and upload documents.

📅 6 & 7. APPOINTMENT & BIOMETRICS

Book an appointment at a VFS France center. Submit your fingerprints, photograph, and physical documents for verification.

💳 8. PAY VISA FEES

Fees range from approx €80 (short stay) to €99 (long stay), plus VFS service charges.

⏳ 9 & 10. PROCESSING & DECISION

The Embassy reviews the file (2–8 weeks). If approved, a visa sticker is affixed to your passport.

✈️ 11 & 12. TRAVEL & RESIDENCE PERMIT

Enter France with your visa and original documents. For long-stays, register with the OFII and apply for your Residence Permit (carte de séjour) at the local prefecture.

🔄 13 & 14. STAY, RENEWAL & PR

Follow your conditions (Work/Study). Permits are renewable annually or multi-year. After 5 years, you become eligible for Permanent Residence and potentially citizenship.

🧠 SUMMARY FLOW: Purpose → Job/Admission → France-Visas portal → VFS Biometrics → Processing → Approval → Travel → OFII/Residence Permit
