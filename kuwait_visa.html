🇰🇼 GENERAL IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR KUWAIT (2026)

Kuwait immigration is primarily sponsor-based (Kafeel system). Almost all long-term stays require a sponsor such as an employer, family member, university, or government entity. Entry usually begins with an Entry Permit, followed by visa stamping, arrival, and residency (Civil ID) issuance.

🟢 THE STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

  • 1. Identify Visa Type: Work (Article 17/18), family, student, investor, visit, domestic worker, etc.
  • 2. Find Sponsor (Kafeel): Employer, family member, university, or government/company.
  • 3. Sponsor Applies in Kuwait: Application submitted to Ministry of Interior (MOI) or Public Authority for Manpower (PAM). Entry Permit is issued if approved.
  • 4. Entry Permit Issued: Pre-approval valid for 1–3 months allowing visa application or travel.
  • 5. Visa Stamping / eVisa: Apply at Kuwait Embassy in India or online. Submit passport, documents, medicals, and fees.
  • 6. Pre-Travel Requirements: GAMCA medical test, police clearance, attested certificates (education/marriage), and travel insurance.
  • 7. Travel to Kuwait: Carry passport, visa, entry permit, and documents.
  • 8. Arrival Clearance: Passport verification and biometric registration (fingerprints/face scan) at the airport.
  • 9. Post-Arrival Formalities: Medical test in Kuwait, fingerprinting, and health insurance activation.
  • 10. Residency Permit (Civil ID): Official residency card issued; required for banking, driving, and work.
  • 11. Stay & Compliance: Must follow visa purpose. No unauthorized employer change. Renew before expiry.
  • 12. Exit / Renewal: Final exit visa for permanent departure or re-entry permit for travel.

🧠 CORE CONCEPTS

  • Kafeel System: Sponsor-based immigration system where employer/family is responsible for the foreigner.
  • Entry Permit: Pre-approval required before visa issuance.
  • Civil ID: Official Kuwait residency identity card.
  • Visa Stamping: Passport visa approval process at embassy.
  • Residency Conversion: Entry permit → visa → residency (Civil ID).
