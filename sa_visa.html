🇸🇦 GENERAL IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR SAUDI ARABIA (2026)

Saudi Arabia immigration is largely sponsor-based (employer, family, government, or institution) except for eVisa categories like tourism, transit, and some special visas. Most long-term visas lead to Iqama (residency permit).

🟢 THE STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

  • 1. Identify Visa Type: Work, family, student, investor, visit, pilgrimage, or special category. Determine if a sponsor is required.
  • 2. Sponsorship / Invitation: Employer, family member, university, or government applies for approval inside Saudi systems (MOFA, Jawazat, MHRSD, MISA).
  • 3. Visa Authorization: Saudi authority issues a visa authorization number or invitation approval. This is mandatory before embassy application.
  • 4. Document Preparation: Passport (6+ months), photos, attested certificates (education, marriage, birth), GAMCA medical report, PCC, and sponsor documents.
  • 5. Application Submission: Apply via Saudi embassy/consulate in India, the online eVisa platform, or approved agents.
  • 6. Verification & Checks: Document verification, security clearance, and medical fitness validation.
  • 7. Visa Issuance / Stamping: Visa stamped on passport or issued as an eVisa. Includes visa number, validity, and entry type.
  • 8. Travel: Book flight and carry passport, visa, insurance, sponsor details, and tickets.
  • 9. Entry Clearance: Passport control, biometric verification (fingerprints/face scan), and entry stamp.
  • 10. Post-Arrival Formalities: Medical test, biometrics registration, issuance of Iqama (residence ID), and insurance activation.
  • 11. Residency Rules: Follow conditions strictly. No unauthorized work. Sponsor is responsible for compliance.
  • 12. Renewal / Exit: Visa renewal based on category, or final exit visa required for permanent departure.

🧠 CORE CONCEPTS

  • Sponsor System: Most Saudi visas require a sponsor responsible for the applicant.
  • Iqama: Saudi residence permit issued after entry for long-term visas.
  • Visa Block Number: Authorization number issued before embassy stamping.
  • eVisa: Electronic visa system for eligible short-term categories.
  • Exit Re-Entry Visa: Permission for residents to leave and return.
