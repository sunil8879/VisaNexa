🇰🇷 GENERAL PROCESS FOR SOUTH KOREA IMMIGRATION

🧭 1. DECIDE YOUR VISA TYPE

Identify the correct category based on your purpose: Short-term (C-3), Study (D-2/D-4), Work (E-series), or Family (F-series).

📩 2. SECURE CORE REQUIREMENTS

Most visas need a sponsor: University Admission (Study), Employment Contract (Work), Investment Proof (D-8), or Marriage Certificate (F-6).

📄 3 & 4. PREPARE DOCUMENTS & SUBMISSION

Standard file: Passport, application form, financial proof (3-6 months statements), and educational certificates. Apply through the Korean Embassy or VFS Global in your country.

🧬 5 & 6. BIOMETRICS & PROCESSING

Submit fingerprints and photos. Interviews are often required for study, work, or marriage visas. Processing takes 5–15 days for short-term and 2–8 weeks for long-term stays.

✅ 7 & 8. APPROVAL & ENTRY

If approved, the visa is stamped in your passport. Travel to South Korea within the validity period and complete immigration inspection at the airport.

🪪 9. ALIEN REGISTRATION CARD (ARC)

MANDATORY: If staying over 90 days, you must register with local immigration within 90 days of arrival to receive your ARC (Residence ID).

🔄 10 & 11. COMPLIANCE & PR PATHWAY

Maintain status, renew before expiry, and follow work/study limits. You may eventually transition to Long-term Residence (F-2) or Permanent Residency (F-5).
