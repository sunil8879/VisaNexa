🇯🇵 GENERAL PROCESS FOR JAPAN IMMIGRATION

🧭 1. IDENTIFY YOUR STATUS OF RESIDENCE

Japan’s system is category-based. You must identify your specific "Status of Residence" before starting: Work (Engineer/Specialist), Student, Business Manager, Highly Skilled Professional (HSP), or Spouse/Dependent.

📩 2. SECURE A JAPANESE SPONSOR

A local sponsor is mandatory for long-term stay. This is typically your Japanese Employer (for work), University (for study), or a family member already residing in Japan. They must support your legal stay.

📄 3 & 4. THE COE (CERTIFICATE OF ELIGIBILITY)

CRITICAL STEP: Your sponsor in Japan applies for the COE at the regional Immigration Services Agency. This document is the government's pre-approval. Processing usually takes 1 to 3 months.

🧬 5 & 6. EMBASSY APPLICATION & PROCESSING

Once you receive the COE, submit your visa application to the Japanese Embassy or VFS Global in India. You will need your Passport, Photo, Visa Form, and the COE. Processing at the Embassy takes 5–15 working days.

✅ 7 & 8. VISA ISSUANCE & ENTRY

If approved, the visa is stamped. You must enter Japan within 3 months of the COE issuance date. At the "Port of Entry" (Airport), an officer will verify your COE and grant your landing permission.

🪪 9. ZAIRYU CARD (RESIDENCE CARD)

MANDATORY: At major airports (Narita, Haneda, Kansai, etc.), you will receive your Zairyu Card immediately upon entry. This is your official ID for all legal activities in Japan.

🔄 10 & 11. MUNICIPAL REGISTRATION & ACTIVITY

Within 14 days of moving into your home, you must visit the local City Hall (Kuyakusho) to register your address. This is also when you enroll in the National Health Insurance and Pension systems.

📈 12. RENEWAL & PR PATHWAY

Status of Residence is usually granted for 1, 3, or 5 years. You must apply for renewal in Japan before expiry. Highly skilled workers may apply for Permanent Residency after 1–3 years via the points-based system.
