Japan Immigration: DIY vs Agency

1. End-to-End Immigration Lifecycle
Technical Glossary:

• COE: Certificate of Eligibility (The mandatory pre-approval document issued in Japan).
• ISA: Immigration Services Agency of Japan (The body that decides your residence status).
• Zairyu Card: Your official Residence Card issued at the airport upon arrival.
• JLPT: Japanese Language Proficiency Test (Level N1 to N5).

🏢 Agency Workflow

Step 1: Status "Advice"Agency checks your degree and suggests a residence status. Often charge for simple "Eligibility check."Time: 2 Days
Step 2: Resume "Japanese-Style"They rewrite your resume into Rirekisho/Shokumu formats. They charge ₹30k+ for basic translation.Time: 1 Week
Step 3: The "Job Match"They claim to have "exclusive tie-ups" with Japanese factories or hotels. They charge a "Placement Bribe."Time: 3-6 Months
Step 4: COE Filing (Middleman)The agency sends your documents to a Japanese "Fixer" who applies for the COE. High risk of fraud here.Time: 1-3 Months
Step 5: COE DeliveryThey send you the COE scan and collect the bulk of your "Visa Fee" (Lakhs).Time: 1 Week
Step 6: VFS StampingThey book your VFS slot. You are overcharged for a visa sticker that costs only ₹500 - ₹3,000.Time: 1 Week
Step 7: Departure BriefingThey give you a "Culture Guide" and tell you what to say if questioned at Narita/Haneda.Time: 1 Day
Step 8: Arrival SupportThey "coordinate" with the sponsor to pick you up. You are now "bound" to that sponsor.Time: 1 Day

🧾 DIY Workflow

Step 1: Status Self-SelectionYou check official ISA.go.jp to see which "Status of Residence" fits your skills.Time: 1 Day
Step 2: DIY Japanese CVYou use free templates for Rirekisho. You emphasize your JLPT levels and technical skills.Time: 2 Days
Step 3: Direct Job HuntYou apply via Daijob, GaijinPot, or LinkedIn.Time: 2-6 Months
Step 4: Official COE FilingYour Japanese employer files for your COE at the regional ISA office. Legally, the company handles this.Time: 1-3 Months
Step 5: Direct COE ReceiptYour HR sends you the digital or physical COE via email/courier. You pay ₹0 for this.Time: Instant
Step 6: Direct VFS FilingYou book your own appointment at VFS Global Japan. You pay only the official low fee.Time: 1 Week
Step 7: Travel BookingYou choose your airline (ANA/JAL/Indigo) and travel to Japan as a documented professional.Time: 1 Week
Step 8: Self-Managed RegistrationYou visit the local Kuyakusho (City Hall) yourself to register your address within 14 days.Time: 1 Day

4. Risk Assessment

DIY RISKS

• Difficulty finding employers from India without Japanese skills.

• Misinterpreting the "Status of Residence" categories.

FINANCIAL RISK: LOW (Govt fees only)

AGENCY RISKS

• Refugee Scam: Agents applying for "Refugee Status" (illegal) instead of a real work visa.

• Kenshusei Trap: Placing professionals in low-wage "Trainee" (TITP) roles.

• Visa Fraud: Using fake degrees to meet the "Specialist in Humanities" criteria.

FINANCIAL RISK: EXTREMELY HIGH
5. Key Success Factors (The Truth)
1. JLPT LevelN3 or N2 is the gold standard. Even N4 increases your hiring odds by 200%.
2. HSP PointsIf you score 70+ points, you get "Highly Skilled Professional" status (PR in 1-3 years).
3. Technical DegreeJapan values Engineers (IT, Mechanical, AI). A degree from a recognized Indian Uni is mandatory.
4. Direct RecruitmentJapanese tech giants (Rakuten, Mercari, Line) only hire through their own portals or top headhunters.
6. Final Conclusion

PROS of DIY

✔ Absolute Legality: No risk of being deported for "visa buying."

✔ Career Growth: You land with a status that matches your real skills.

✔ Savings: ₹12 Lakhs is enough to live in Tokyo for 18 months.

CONS of AGENCY

✖ High Debt: Working for 2 years just to pay off the agent's markup.

✖ Limited Options: Agents only work with small firms that can't find talent.

✖ Fraud Vulnerability: You are responsible for any fake info the agent submits.

FINAL VERDICT: Japan is an "Employer-Led" system. A real Japanese job offer costs the applicant ZERO. Spend your money on Japanese language classes, not on an agent's commission.
