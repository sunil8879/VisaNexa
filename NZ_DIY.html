New Zealand PR: DIY vs AGENCY TRUTH

🚨 THE NZ JOB-OFFER TRAP

New Zealand is a highly "Employer-Driven" market. Without a valid job offer from an accredited employer, your points mean very little. Agencies often promise "Guaranteed Jobs" in NZ for ₹20 Lakhs—these are usually low-wage roles that do not qualify for PR.

1. End-to-End Immigration Lifecycle
Technical Glossary:

• NZQA: NZ Qualifications Authority (Verifies if your degree matches NZ standards).
• EOI: Expression of Interest (The first step in the points-based system).
• ITA: Invitation to Apply (The official go-ahead from the government to file your visa).
• Green List: A list of high-demand occupations that have a fast-track to residency.

🏢 Agency Workflow

Step 1: Points EstimationAgency calculates your points based on age and degree. They often inflate the score to sign you up.Time: 2 Days
Step 2: CV "NZ-ification"They rewrite your resume using generic NZ templates. They charge a "Premium Branding Fee" for this.Time: 1 Week
Step 3: NZQA HandlingThey file your degree verification with NZQA. You pay the fee + their service charge.Time: 4-8 Weeks
Step 4: The "Job Search"They claim to apply for you on Seek/TradeMe. In reality, they just send automated emails that employers ignore.Time: 3-6 Months
Step 5: EOI SubmissionAgency files your EOI via INZ. They keep the login secret from you.Time: 1 Week
Step 6: ITA ResponseIf you get an invitation, they handle the complex document upload to justify their 20 Lakh fee.Time: 4 Weeks
Step 7: Medical/Police CheckThey guide you on which doctors to visit. You pay the doctors directly anyway.Time: 2 Weeks
Step 8: Final Visa TrackingThey monitor the 'Immigration NZ' portal for the final grant.Time: 3-6 Months

🧾 DIY Workflow

Step 1: Green List CheckYou check if your job is on the INZ Green List yourself for free.Time: 1 Day
Step 2: DIY NZ-Style CVYou create a "Clean & Direct" NZ CV focusing on technical skills and local certifications.Time: 2 Days
Step 3: Direct NZQA FilingYou upload your degree directly to NZQA.govt.nz and track the progress yourself.Time: 4-8 Weeks
Step 4: Active Job HuntingYou apply directly via Seek.co.nz or LinkedIn. You talk directly to hiring managers.Time: 2-6 Months
Step 5: EOI Portal FilingYou create your own EOI account. You pay the official fee directly. You own the password.Time: 1 Day
Step 6: Direct ITA FilingWhen invited, you upload your original documents yourself. No "middleman markup" on fees.Time: 2 Weeks
Step 7: Clear Health/CharacterYou book your own Panel Physician and PCC at the Passport Seva Kendra.Time: 2 Weeks
Step 8: Self-TrackingYou get the 'Visa Granted' email directly in your inbox. No agency to hide it from you.Time: 3-6 Months

3. Timeline & Critical Risks

TIMELINE

• NZQA: 1-2 months

• Job Offer: 2-6 months (Critical Stage)

• EOI to ITA: 1 month

• Visa Processing: 3-8 months

Note: No agent has a "fast track" with Immigration NZ. They all use the same queue.

AGENCY RISKS

• Fake Job Offers: Agencies create fake companies to issue job offers.

• Low-Skill Trap: Placing you in jobs that don't meet the "Skilled" threshold for PR.

• Financial Ruin: Losing ₹15L without a visa is common.

4. What Actually Decides Success in NZ?
1. The "Green List"If you are a Nurse, Engineer, or ICT expert, you can get PR almost immediately (Straight to Residence).
2. Accredited EmployerYour job offer MUST be from a company registered as an "Accredited Employer" with INZ.
3. Median WageYour salary must meet the NZ Median Wage threshold (currently ~$31.61/hr) to be "Skilled."
4. English ScoreA high PTE/IELTS score is mandatory; there is no way for an agent to "skip" this.
5. Final Verdict

THE DIY PATH

✔ Honest: You talk to real employers who value your skills.

✔ Rich: You keep ₹18 Lakhs in your pocket for NZ living costs.

✔ Secure: 0% risk of visa fraud/ban.

THE AGENCY PATH

✖ Exploitative: You pay for a "service" that is free on the govt website.

✖ Dangerous: If the "Job Spot" is found to be bought, you get deported.

✖ Blind: You have no idea what the agent is telling the government.

FINAL VERDICT: In New Zealand, your Job Offer is your Visa. Employers hire based on your talent, not on who your agent is. Use your money for your flight and settlement, not to pay a middleman's commission.
