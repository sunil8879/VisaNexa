🇮🇹 GENERAL PROCESS FOR ITALY IMMIGRATION

🎯 PHASE 1: DECIDE VISA TYPE

Identify your purpose: Work (Salaried/Blue Card), Study (University), Business (Entrepreneur), or Family. This decides if you need a National Visa Type D (long stay) or Schengen Type C (short stay).

🛡️ PHASE 2: GET PRE-APPROVAL (NULLA OSTA)

For most long-stay visas (especially Work and Family), you must obtain the Nulla Osta (Authorization). Your employer or family member in Italy must apply for this at the Sportello Unico per l’Immigrazione (SUI) before you apply for the visa.

🧾 PHASE 3 & 4: DOCUMENTATION & APPOINTMENT

Prepare your core file: Passport, application form, financial proof, and your Nulla Osta. Book your biometric and submission appointment through VFS Global Italy in India.

🧬 PHASE 5 & 6: APPLICATION & PROCESSING

Attend your appointment at VFS. The Italian Embassy verifies documents and may call for an interview. Processing takes 2 weeks to 3 months depending on the category.

✈️ PHASE 7 & 8: DECISION & TRAVEL

If approved, a Type D visa sticker is issued. Travel to Italy and ensure you carry all original documents, especially the Nulla Osta.

🏠 PHASE 9 & 10: RESIDENCE PERMIT (PERMESSO DI SOGGIORNO)

CRITICAL: Within 8 days of arrival, you must apply for your residence permit at a local Poste Italiane (Yellow post office). You will then receive an appointment at the Questura (Police HQ) for fingerprints.

🪪 PHASE 11 & 12: TAX ID & RENEWAL

Obtain your Codice Fiscale (Tax ID) to open a bank account and register for healthcare. Renew your permit before it expires. After 5 years of legal stay, you can apply for PR (EU Long-Term Residence).

🧠 SUMMARY FLOW: Choose Visa → Get Nulla Osta (if needed) → VFS Application → Get Visa → Travel → Apply Residence Permit at Post Office → Questura Appointment → Start Work/Study
