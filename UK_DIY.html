UK Skilled Worker: DIY vs AGENCY

🚨 THE UK SPONSORSHIP REALITY

In the UK, you cannot "buy" a visa. You must have a job offer from a 'Licensed Sponsor'. Many agencies "sell" you Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) numbers from shelf companies—which is illegal and leads to immediate deportation.

1. End-to-End UK Immigration Workflow
Technical Glossary:

• CoS: Certificate of Sponsorship (A digital reference number from your employer).
• IHS: Immigration Health Surcharge (Fee to use the NHS, approx £1,035/year).
• Licensed Sponsor: A UK company approved by the Home Office to hire foreigners.
• OET/IELTS: Mandatory English proficiency tests for UK visa applicants.

🏢 Agency Workflow

Step 1: Point CalculationAgency checks if you meet the 70-point threshold (Job + Salary + English).Time: 2 Days
Step 2: Resume "Refurbishing"They edit your CV to match UK keywords. Often they exaggerate experience to fit 'Shortage Occupations'.Time: 1 Week
Step 3: The "Job Match"They claim to have "internal links" with UK Care Homes or IT firms. They often charge a bribe for this.Time: 1-3 Months
Step 4: CoS AcquisitionThe agency manages the employer to issue your CoS. You are often not told the real salary on the CoS.Time: 2-4 Weeks
Step 5: IHS & Visa PaymentThey ask you to transfer the IHS and Visa fees to THEIR account, often adding a markup.Time: 1 Day
Step 6: Visa SubmissionAgency fills the form on GOV.UK. You rarely get the login credentials.Time: 1 Week
Step 7: VFS AppointmentThey book your biometrics and tell you what to say if the officer calls for a 'Credibility Interview'.Time: 2 Weeks
Step 8: TrackingThey track the BRP (Biometric Residence Permit) delivery.Time: 3-8 Weeks

🧾 DIY Workflow

Step 1: Sponsor List CheckYou download the official "Register of Licensed Sponsors" from GOV.UK and target those firms.Time: 1 Day
Step 2: UK-Standard CVYou create a 2-page CV with a strong "Personal Statement" (no photo/DOB as per UK law).Time: 2 Days
Step 3: Direct ApplicationsYou apply via LinkedIn UK, Indeed UK, or directly on the NHS Jobs portal.Time: 1-4 Months
Step 4: Real Job OfferYou crack technical/clinical interviews. The employer issues a 100% legal, free CoS to you.Time: 2 Weeks
Step 5: Direct Fee PaymentYou pay the IHS and Visa fee directly on the Home Office portal using your own card.Time: 1 Hour
Step 6: GOV.UK FilingYou fill the application yourself. You own the login. You know exactly what data is submitted.Time: 2 Days
Step 7: Biometrics (VFS)You visit VFS Global yourself. You are confident because your job offer is real.Time: 2 Weeks
Step 8: BRP CollectionYou receive your passport with the vignette and collect your BRP once you land in the UK.Time: 3-8 Weeks

3. Processing & Critical Risks

TIMELINE

• Job Search: 2-6 months (Depends on skills)

• CoS Issuance: 1-4 weeks (Digital)

• Visa Priority: 5 working days (Extra cost)

• Standard Visa: 3 weeks

Note: An agency cannot speed up the UKVI decision time.

THE "CARE" SCAM WARNING

• Fake CoS: Selling sponsorship for non-existent care jobs.

• Salary Clawback: Employer pays you, then takes cash back under the table.

• Sponsor Revocation: If the Home Office raids the employer, your visa is cancelled in 60 days.

4. Success Pillars (The UK Formula)
1. NHS PathwaysNurses and doctors have a 99% success rate via direct NHS applications.
2. Salary ThresholdYou must earn at least £38,700 (standard) or £29,000 (Health/Care) to qualify.
3. Shortage SkillsEngineers, Programmers, and Social Workers are prioritized.
4. Genuine VacancyThe Home Office checks if the job is real or just created to sell a visa.
5. Final Conclusion

PROS of DIY

✔ Absolute Safety: No risk of being banned for fraud.

✔ Direct NHS hiring is the "Gold Standard" for Indians.

✔ Saves ₹10L - ₹15L which can be used for UK housing.

CONS of AGENCY

✖ You are often tied to "shady" employers who exploit workers.

✖ High risk of "Sponsor Licence Revocation" after you land.

✖ No refund if the Home Office rejects the CoS.

FINAL VERDICT: For Healthcare professionals, NEVER use an agency—NHS recruits directly. For others, DIY ensures your UK career starts on legal ground, not on a "bought" job offer that can collapse any day.
