🇷🇴 GENERAL IMMIGRATION PROCESS FOR ROMANIA (2026)

Romania immigration is managed through a tiered system: initial approval/invitation, followed by a Long-Stay (D) visa, and finally a Residence Permit (IGI) after entry.

🧭 1. DECIDE YOUR VISA CATEGORY

  • Study (D/SD): For University or academic programs.
  • Work (D/AM / Blue Card): For employment by Romanian companies.
  • Business (D/AC): For commercial and investment activities.
  • Family (D/VF): For reunification with family in Romania.
  • Special: Digital nomad, research, or training.

📩 2. GET INVITATION / APPROVAL

Most long-stay visas require prior approval from within Romania: an Admission Letter (Student), a Work Permit (Worker), or Corporate Documents (Business).

📄 3. APPLY FOR LONG-STAY VISA (D VISA)

Submit your application at the Romanian Embassy or Consulate. Standard core file includes: Passport (valid 6+ months), application form, insurance (€30k coverage), and your official Romanian invitation/permit.

⏳ 4 & 5. PROCESSING & DECISION

Processing typically takes 10–45 working days. If approved, you receive a D-type long-stay visa, usually valid for 90 days for entry.

✈️ 6 & 7. TRAVEL & RESIDENCE PERMIT (IGI)

Travel to Romania. Crucial: Within 30 days of arrival, you must apply for a Residence Permit at the Romanian Immigration Office (IGI) for biometrics and address registration.

🪪 8 & 9. PERMIT ISSUANCE & RENEWAL

Receive your Residence Permit card (usually valid for 1 year). This must be renewed in Romania before expiry based on your continued activity (job/study).

🇪🇺 10. LONG-TERM SETTLEMENT

After 5 years of continuous legal residence, you become eligible for Permanent Residence (PR) or EU long-term status, and eventually Citizenship.

🧠 SUMMARY FLOW: Offer/Invitation → D Visa → Travel → IGI Residence Permit → Renewal → PR/Citizenship
